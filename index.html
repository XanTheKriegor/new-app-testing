✦ How to Use ✦
▶ Creating a character
Tap + New Character and fill in name, class, level, race and alignment. Sound Set is optional, you can leave it blank and set it up later. Once saved the character shows up on the main screen, tap it any time to open it again.
▶ Spell slots
Open a character and tap Spellbook. Each row shows the slots for that level as dots, tap one to mark it used or available again if you need to fix something by hand. Long Rest refills everything. Short Rest only helps Warlocks, since Pact Magic is the only thing that comes back early. Slots get recalculated automatically whenever you level up a character.
▶ Learning and preparing spells
All Spells is the full database, filtered to your class by default. Tap Add to Book on anything you want to learn and it moves into Known Spells. From there tap Prepare to bring it into Prepared Spells, which is the list you actually cast from during play.
▶ Casting
Hit Cast on a prepared spell. If it can be cast with more than one slot level you'll get asked which one to burn. Cantrips skip slots entirely. If nothing's free at a high enough level you'll just get a heads up instead of it silently failing.
▶ Spell card details
Cards start collapsed, showing just the name, level and school. Tap the name to expand range, duration, casting time, components, source and classes. Tap Description separately to read the full text, it's independent from the rest of the details. If you'd rather have everything expanded by default, there's a Details Off/On toggle next to the filter button in the spellbook that applies to every card at once.
▶ Pinning spells
Tap the star on a prepared spell to pin it. Pinned spells jump to the top of the Prepared list so the ones you actually rely on in combat aren't buried under cantrips and rituals. Tap the star again to unpin.
▶ Concentration
There's a small bar above the spell tabs that shows what you're currently concentrating on, or tells you there's nothing active. Casting a new concentration spell while one is already running will ask you to confirm before swapping it out, just like the actual rules. You can also break it manually any time with the button on the bar.
▶ Search and filters
The search box matches by name. Tap ⚙ Filters for level, school, class, source book, ritual and concentration, all of which stack together and apply across Prepared, Known and All Spells alike.
▶ Spell sounds
Set a Sound Set on a character for a default sound per school of magic whenever you cast. Tap 📁 Sounds once per session to point the app at your local sounds folder. Any individual prepared spell can also get its own custom sound through the 🎵 button on its card, which takes priority over the default.
▶ Light and dark mode
Switch between the two with the buttons above the title on the main screen. The choice is saved and applies everywhere in the app, not just the page you're on.
▶ Backup and transfer
Everything lives in your browser's local storage, which doesn't follow you to a new device or browser on its own. Use ⬇ Export on the main screen to save a backup file, and ⬆ Import wherever you want it restored. Characters, known and prepared spells, slot states and custom sounds all come back with it.
